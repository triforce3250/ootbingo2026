Tournament Rules & Format

Unless there is a serious oversight or issue, no changes to the information or rules below will be made after signup closes.

General Information

  • Sign-up Deadline: Closes on Friday, August 28th, at 11:59pm US-Western (UTC-7). Sign up via the Bingo Tournament Discord.
  • Timeline: The tournament officially starts on Sunday, August 30th. Pairings for the first round may be determined after signup closes but before this date.
  • Game Version: Played on the latest version of Ocarina of Time Bingo (currently v10.6). The version may be updated between rounds, but never while a round is actively in progress.
  • Structure: Consists of 2 phases: a 3-week Groups phase followed by a 4-5 week single-elimination bracket. Seeding races take place during the final week of signups.
  • Attendance: Entrants must plan to be available for one match per week. Not all entrants will qualify for the single-elimination bracket phase.
  • Requirements to Join:
    • A Racetime.gg account with at least 1 completed race in the OoT Bingo category (at any time ever). You may sign up before fulfilling this, but must finish a race before signups close (e.g., via ESNB/SNB).
    • The ability to stream your gameplay livestreamed on Twitch or a similar platform.

General Match Rules

  • Game Versions & Emulators: Allowed on any officially released version of the game (typically Japanese for faster text; iQue is excluded). Emulators are allowed (Dolphin [recommended] or Project64 v1.6/1.7/3.0) but must use the configurations from the OoT Speedrun.com rules.
  • Banned Tricks: See the sidebar of any Bingo card to view rules on goal specifications and banned major version-specific tricks (such as SRM, GIM, or Stick/QPA as Adult).
  • Race Rooms & Reporting: All matches must be played on Racetime.gg in a separate, 2-entrant race room. They must be announced in the tournament Discord's #race-announcements channel right before starting.
  • Scheduling Courtesy: If possible, players are encouraged to avoid overlapping their matches with other scheduled tournament races or the weekly Saturday races.
  • Restreams & Spoilers: Matches may be restreamed on the OoTV Twitch channel. Entrants may not watch the restream or read the #race-spoilers channel while racing. Watching your direct opponent's stream is allowed.
  • Match Restarts: Allowed only if:
    1. BingoBot fails to generate a board when the race starts (restart in a new room).
    2. An entrant's game crashes due to external factors (excluding intentional softlocks), and both entrants agree to restart. No player is obligated to accept a restart.

Providing Help & Goal Validation

  • Discord Help Channel: Groups phase ONLY. Entrants may ask goal-oriented questions in #bingo-help during a race.
    • Allowed: "Does X goal require ZL?", "Is there a trap for X goal?", "Can someone link a setup for X trick?"
    • Banned: "Which row should I pick?", "Should I go back in time?", "Can someone check what I am doing wrong?"
  • Unsolicited Help: It is strictly prohibited to provide unsolicited help to racing players. Please do not answer questions in an entrant's Twitch chat to prevent giving an unfair advantage.
  • Goal Errors: If an entrant misses a requirement for a goal (e.g., forgetting or deleting an item), mention it in the Racetime room AFTER they finish. You may only state which goal was incomplete, not what they missed. Validation can be undone via Racetime for up to 10 minutes post-race.

Handling Ties & Retiming

  • Popout Window Capture: Players should attempt to capture the Racetime room popout window (specifically the timer) to align the race start with stream VOD footage.
  • Draws: In the rare event of a literal tie down to the second, VODs will be compared. If only one player captured the popout window, they are declared the winner. If neither did, the match must be replayed. In the Groups phase, both match times are used for Swiss round calculations.
  • Lag & Retiming: If a race finish is within 10 seconds and players experience lag or an unclear finish moment, TOs may retime the match. This requires players to have had the Racetime timer visible on screen at the start of the race.

Groups Phase: King of the Hill Format

  • The Groups phase consists of 3 rounds. Each round lasts exactly one week and must be finished by Monday at 8PM UTC.
  • If all matches in a round finish early, pairings for the next round may be generated early so players can begin scheduling.
  • Finalizing Standings: After Round 3, ranked groups of up to 8 players are finalized. The median time within those groups determines final tournament ranking.
  • Bracket Qualification Rules:
    • Fewer than 32 entrants: Top 12 players (the top group + top half of the second group) get seeds 1–12. The remaining players compete via the top 4 median times for seeds 13–16.
    • 32 or more entrants: Top 20 players (the top 2 groups + top half of the third group) get seeds 1–20. The remaining players compete via the top 4 median times for seeds 21–24.
  • Deadlines & Forfeits: Unfinished matches past the weekly deadline will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis by tournament organizers. A forfeit or any completed time greater than 4 hours will automatically count as a 4:00:00 time.

Bracket Phase: Single-Elimination

  • The final phase is a single-elimination bracket where every match is a Best of 1.
  • Pairings: Seeding is directly pulled from the Groups phase ranking. Highest seed plays lowest seed (1 vs 16, 2 vs 15, etc.).
  • Rematch Prevention: To avoid direct rematches from the groups phase in Round 1 of the bracket, the tournament coordinators will swap the lower-seeded player with the next closest seed.

TL;DR

Entrants will be ranked into groups of 8 each round. After each round, the top 2 winners move to the above group, and the bottom 2 losers move to the lower group. At the end, entrants are ranked by median time within groups. Don't worry too much about it—always good to win, but getting good times can be more important!

What is this format?

Rather than a Swiss Rounds format, this year's bracket qualifying phase features a "King of the Hill" (Promotion & Relegation) format. Entrants are broken into groups of 8, with each group divided into a top half and a lower half. The top half faces the lower half, and players dynamically move between groups each round depending on their specific match results and times.

The goal: Have exciting matches between players of similar skill much sooner, ultimately producing a similar ranking to a Swiss or Round Robin format for the single elimination bracket.

How Matches Are Determined

  • Round 1 Pairings: Initial groups and seeds are determined directly by the pre-tournament seeding races. Within each group, pairings follow a traditional structure: Rank 1 vs Rank 8, Rank 2 vs Rank 7, Rank 3 vs Rank 6, and Rank 4 vs Rank 5.
  • Subsequent Rounds: Within each group, the top half is ranked by their previous round time and faces the bottom half (also ranked by their previous round time).

Bracket Qualification Mechanics

At the conclusion of 3 rounds, the final groups are determined. Entrants are ranked by their final group, and then by their median time across the matches to determine seeding for the single-elimination bracket:

  • If under 32 entrants: The top 12 players (from the entire top group and the top half of the second group) are seeds 1–12. The top 4 remaining median times are seeds 13-16.
  • If 32 or more entrants: The top 20 players (comprising the top 2 groups and the top half of the third group) are seeds 1–20. The top 4 remaining median times are seeds 21-24.

Edge Cases & Technicalities

  • Size Anomalies (Not Divisible by 8): If the number of entrants is not divisible by 8, the bottom group(s) will adapt to be smaller (retaining a minimum threshold of 4 players), applying similar rules for promotion and relegation (top and bottom halves but with fewer players).
  • Odd Player Counts (Byes): If there are an odd number of entrants, the lowest group will have an odd number. Each round, the highest-ranked player in that specific group receives a bye. Note: Players on a bye must still run a race to have a time for that round, and no player will receive a bye more than once.
  • Rematch Restrictions: Due to the nature of the groups phase, rematches can occur. Groups phase rematches will be explicitly prevented during Round 1 of the bracket.
  • Handling Forfeits & DNFs: A forfeit or DNF translates to a fixed 4:00:00 time value. If more than two losers DNF in a single group, all DNF instances shift downward, and an equal number of qualifying winners from the group below will promote to fill the vacancies.
  • DNF Tiebreakers: To properly rank players who DNF for the subsequent round's pairings, the player's overall tournament median up to that exact juncture is utilized. If still tied, the original Round 1 seeding is used as the baseline fallback.
  • Double DNFs: If both active racers in a match DNF, the player who physically DNF'd first receives the match loss. The player who DNF'd second receives the match win. Both racers receive a 4:00:00 time penalty applied to their tracking metrics.
  • Legitimate Match Restarts: If an explicit restart is triggered under the rules (e.g., BingoBot error or agreed upon restart due to console crash), both players should DNF inside the Racetime room and create a new room. This will not count as a DNF toward the tournament median.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I sign up?

Sign up by reacting to an announcement post in the Bingo Tournament Discord, before August 28th at 11:59PM US-Western (UTC-7).

Who can join this tournament?

Anyone who has completed at least 1 race on Racetime (ever) before signup closes can join. You will need to be available for 1 match a week, starting in the last week of August for 3 weeks. The top 16/24 bracket stage will last 4-5 weeks after this.

I'll be unavailable for a week and have to miss a match. Is that okay?

The results of the Groups phase will include the median of 3 times. So, if you skip 1, your median will be the lower of the remaining 2 times. This means it is possible to miss a week, but you must let the Tournament Organizers know ahead of time so we can plan the week's pairings accordingly.

Can I ask for help during tournament matches?

During the Groups stage only, you can ask for help through the #bingo-help channel of the tournament Discord. Check the specific section in the Rules & Format tab on providing help for more details on exactly what types of questions are allowed.

Are there practice races?

Yes! Typically players ping the @bingo role in the OoT Speedrunning Discord within the #races channel to find casual matches. There are also 2 weekly scheduled community races on Saturdays (look out for @bingo pings for ESNB/SNB).

I don't have a console to play OoT. Can I use an emulator?

Yes, and emulators are often a quicker and easier way to get started! Certain emulators are allowed (Dolphin and Project64 v1.6/1.7/3.0) but must use the configurations from OoT's Speedrun.com rules. The Ocarina of Time speedrunning Discord can also help with general setup.

Bingo seems too hard for me. Should I still join?

Bingo is hard, but it gets easier every single time! It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed and have no idea what to do or what each goal means at first. A great recommendation is to learn a small number of baseline tricks which you'll need every time and will save massive amounts of time (Forest Escape, Door of Time skip, a little bit of Reverse Bottle Adventure, and Early Shadow & Spirit Temple), and to focus on finding just one doable row on a test board. Over time you'll naturally learn more goals and more rows will open up. You can do it!

How can I learn Bingo?

There are many excellent resources out there to learn, ranging from foundational guides to advanced routing. Join the BingBros Discord to view the community resource lists (these are also in the #tutorials channel in the Tournament Discord). If you want one video to start, check out the Top 5 Essential Tricks. Don't be afraid to ask questions—everyone is happy to help!
