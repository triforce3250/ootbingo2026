Tournament Rules & Format

Unless there is a serious oversight or issue, no changes to the information or rules below will be made after signup closes.

General Information

  • Signup Deadline: Closes on Saturday, August 29th, at 11:59pm US-Eastern (UTC-4). Sign up via the Bingo Tournament Discord.
  • Tournament Start: The tournament officially starts on Sunday, August 30th. Pairings for the first round (and all future rounds) will be announced on OoTV and in the tournament discord.
  • Bingo Version: Played on the latest version of Ocarina of Time Bingo (currently v10.6). The version may be updated between rounds, but never while a round is actively in progress.
  • Structure: Consists of 2 phases: a 3-week Groups phase followed by a Top 16/24 bracket. Seeding races take place during the final week of signups.
  • Attendance: Entrants must plan to be available for one match per week. Not all entrants will qualify for the bracket phase.
  • Requirements to Join:
    • A Racetime.gg account with at least 1 completed race in the OoT Bingo category (at any time ever). You may sign up before fulfilling this, but must finish a race before signups close (e.g., via ESNB/SNB).
    • The ability to stream your gameplay livestreamed on Twitch or a similar platform.

General Match Rules

  • Game Versions & Emulators: Allowed on any officially released version of the game (typically Japanese for faster text; iQue is excluded). Emulators are allowed (Dolphin [recommended] or Project64 v1.6/1.7/3.0) but must use the configurations from the OoT Speedrun.com rules.
  • Banned Tricks: See the sidebar of any Bingo card to view rules on goal specifications and banned major version-specific tricks (such as SRM, GIM, and Stick/QPA as Adult).
  • Race Rooms & Reporting: All matches must be played on Racetime.gg in a separate, 2-entrant race room. They must be announced in the tournament Discord's #race-announcements channel right before starting.
  • Scheduling Courtesy: If possible, players are encouraged to avoid overlapping their matches with other scheduled tournament races or the weekly Saturday races.
  • Restreams & Spoilers: Matches may be restreamed on the OoTV Twitch channel. Entrants may not watch the restream or read the #race-spoilers channel while racing. Watching your direct opponent's stream is allowed.
  • Match Restarts: Allowed only if:
    1. BingoBot fails to generate a board when the race starts (restart in a new room).
    2. An entrant's game crashes due to external factors (excluding intentional softlocks), and both entrants agree to restart. No player is obligated to accept a restart.

Providing Help & Goal Validation

  • Discord Help Channel: Groups phase ONLY. Entrants may ask goal-oriented questions in #bingo-help during a race.
    • Allowed: "Does X goal require ZL?", "Is there a trap for X goal?", "Can someone link a setup for X trick?"
    • Banned: "Which row should I pick?", "Should I go back in time?", "Can someone check what I am doing wrong?"
  • Unsolicited Help: It is strictly prohibited to provide unsolicited help to racing players. Please do not answer questions in an entrant's Twitch chat to prevent giving an unfair advantage.
  • Goal Errors: If an entrant misses a requirement for a goal (e.g., forgetting or deleting an item), mention it in the Racetime room AFTER they finish. You may only state which goal was incomplete, not what they missed. Finished races can be undone via Racetime for up to 10 minutes post-race.

Handling Ties & Retiming

  • Popout Window Capture: Players should attempt to capture the Racetime room popout window (specifically the timer) to align the race start with stream VOD footage.
  • Draws: In the rare event of a literal tie down to the second, VODs will be compared. If only one player captured the popout window, they are declared the winner. If neither did, the match must be replayed. In the Groups phase, both match times are used as part of the tournament median.
  • Racetime Lag & Retiming: If a race finish is within 10 seconds and players experience lag on Racetime.gg or an unclear finish moment, TOs may retime the match. This requires players to have had the Racetime timer visible on screen at the start of the race.

Groups Phase: "King of the Hill" / Promotion & Relegation Format

  • The Groups phase consists of 3 rounds. Each round lasts exactly one week and must be finished by Monday at 6PM UTC.
  • If all matches in a round finish early, pairings for the next round may be determined early so players can begin scheduling.
  • Finalizing Standings: After Round 3, ranked groups of up to 8 players are finalized. The median time (from all Groups rounds) within those groups determines the seeding for the top 16/24 bracket.
  • Bracket Qualification Rules:
    • Fewer than 32 entrants: Top 12 players (the top group + top half of the second group) get seeds 1–12. The top 4 remaining median times are seeds 13-16.
    • 32 or more entrants: Top 20 players (the top 2 groups + top half of the third group) get seeds 1–20. The top 4 remaining median times are seeds 21-24.
  • Deadlines & Forfeits: Unfinished matches past the weekly deadline will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis by tournament organizers. A forfeit or any completed time greater than 4 hours will automatically count as a 4:00:00 time.

Bracket Phase: Hybrid Top 16/24 Bracket

  • Format: (New!) The final bracket is a mix of single-elimination and double-elimination starting in the Quarterfinals (Top 8) round. Every match is a Best of 1 and must be completed within a week.
  • Losers Matches (New!) The Losers Bracket matches are winner-takes-all Free-For-All matches. They will take place by default on Saturdays at 15:00 UTC (11 AM US-Eastern), if players cannot agree on a preferred time.
  • Initial Pairings: Seeding for the initial round is determined by the Groups phase ranking. Highest seed plays lowest seed (1 vs 16, 2 vs 15, etc.).
  • Rematch Prevention: To avoid direct rematches from the Groups phase in the first round of the bracket, the tournament organizers will swap the lower-seeded player with the next highest seed.

TL;DR

Entrants will be ranked into groups of 8 each round. After each round, the top 2 winners move to the above group, and the bottom 2 losers move to the lower group. At the end, entrants are ranked by median time within groups. Don't worry too much about it—always good to win, but getting good times can be more important!

What is this format?

Rather than a Swiss Rounds format, this year's bracket qualifying phase will again be a "King of the Hill" (Promotion & Relegation) format. Entrants are broken into groups of 8, with each group divided into a top half and a lower half. The top half faces the lower half, and players dynamically move between groups each round depending on their specific match results and times.

The goal: Have exciting matches between players of similar skill much sooner, ultimately producing a similar ranking to a Swiss or Round Robin format for the bracket.

How Matches Are Determined

  • Round 1 Pairings: Initial groups and seeds are determined directly by the pre-tournament seeding races. Within each group, pairings follow a traditional structure: Rank 1 vs Rank 8, Rank 2 vs Rank 7, Rank 3 vs Rank 6, and Rank 4 vs Rank 5.
  • Subsequent Rounds: Within each group, the top half is ranked by their previous round time and faces the bottom half (also ranked by their previous round time).

Bracket Qualification Mechanics

At the conclusion of 3 rounds, the final groups are determined. Entrants are ranked by their final group, and then by their median time across the matches to determine seeding for the bracket:

  • If under 32 entrants: The top 12 players (from the entire top group and the top half of the second group) are seeds 1–12. The top 4 remaining median times are seeds 13-16.
  • If 32 or more entrants: The top 20 players (comprising the top 2 groups and the top half of the third group) are seeds 1–20. The top 4 remaining median times are seeds 21-24.

Edge Cases & Technicalities

  • Group Size Anomalies: If the number of entrants is not divisible by 8, the bottom group(s) will adapt to be smaller (while still having at least 4 entrants), applying similar rules for promotion and relegation (top and bottom halves but with fewer players).
  • Odd Entrant Count: If there are an odd number of entrants, the lowest group will have an odd number. Each round, the highest-ranked entrant in that group receives a bye. Players on a bye must still run a race during that week (and announce it in #race-announcements) to have a time for that round. No player will receive a bye in the Groups phase more than once.
  • Rematch Restrictions: When possible, rematches will be avoided in the Groups phase by switching the lower ranked player with the next highest player. Groups phase rematches will be explicitly prevented during Round 1 of the bracket by the same method.
  • Handling Forfeits & DNFs: A forfeit / DNF will be counted as a 4:00:00 time for the tournament median. If more than two entrants DNF in a single group, they will all move down to the lower group, and an equal number of qualifying winners from the group below will promote.
  • DNF Tiebreakers: To properly rank players who DNF for the subsequent round's pairings, the player's overall tournament median up to that point will be used. If still tied, the original Round 1 seeding will be used.
  • Double DNFs: If both active racers in a match DNF, the player who DNF'd first receives the match loss. The player who DNF'd second receives the match win. Both racers receive a 4:00:00 time for the round in this case.
  • Legitimate Match Restarts: If an explicit restart is triggered under the rules (e.g., BingoBot error or agreed upon restart due to console crash), both players should DNF inside the Racetime room and create a new room (with new board). This will not count as a DNF toward the tournament median.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I sign up?

Sign up by reacting to an announcement post in the Bingo Tournament Discord, before August 29th at 11:59PM US-Eastern (UTC-4).

Who can join this tournament?

Anyone who has completed at least 1 OoT Bingo race on Racetime (ever) before signup closes can join. You will need to be available for 1 match a week, starting at the end of August for 3 weeks. The top 16/24 bracket will last 6-8 weeks after this.

I'll be unavailable for a week and have to miss a match. Is that okay?

The results of the Groups phase will include the median of 3 times. So, if you skip 1, your median will be the lower of the remaining 2 times. This means it is possible to miss a week, but you must let the Tournament Organizers know ahead of time so we can plan the week's pairings accordingly.

Can I ask for help during tournament matches?

During the Groups phase only, you can ask for help through the #bingo-help channel of the tournament Discord. Check the specific section in the Rules & Format tab on providing help for more details on exactly what types of questions are allowed.

Are there practice races?

Yes! Typically players ping the @bingo role in the OoT Speedrunning Discord within the #races channel to find casual matches. There are also 2 weekly scheduled community races on Saturdays (look out for @bingo pings for ESNB/SNB).

I don't have a console to play OoT. Can I use an emulator?

Yes, and emulators are often a quicker and easier way to get started! Certain emulators are allowed (Dolphin and Project64 v1.6/1.7/3.0) but must use the configurations from OoT's Speedrun.com rules. The Ocarina of Time speedrunning Discord can also help with general setup.

Bingo seems too hard for me. Should I still join?

Bingo is hard, but it gets easier every single time! It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed and have no idea what to do or what each goal means at first. A great recommendation is to learn a small number of baseline tricks which you'll need every time and will save massive amounts of time (Forest Escape, Door of Time skip, a little bit of Reverse Bottle Adventure, and Early Shadow & Spirit Temple), and to focus on finding just one doable row on a test board. Over time you'll naturally learn more goals and more rows will open up. You can do it!

How can I learn Bingo?

There are many excellent resources out there to learn, ranging from foundational guides to advanced routing. Join the BingBros Discord to view the community resource lists (these are also in the #tutorials channel in the Tournament Discord). If you want one video to start, check out the Top 5 Essential Tricks. Don't be afraid to ask questions—everyone is happy to help!
